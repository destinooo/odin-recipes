Brookie Recipe

Thick and Soft Brookies

Delightful dessert that combines the best of both worlds—brownie and cookie! It features a fudgy, ich brownie base topped with a chewy, sweet cookie layer. The two textures come together in a perfect, decadent treat that's a sure hit for anyone who loves both brownies and cookies.

Ingredients:

  • For the Brownie Layer:
    • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted
    • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1/3 cup (40g) unsweentened cocoa powder
    • 1/2 cup (60g) all purpose flour
    • 1/4 tsp salt
  • For the Cookie Layer:
    • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
    • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
    • 1/2 cup (90g) brown sugar, packed
    • 1 large egg
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1 1/2 cups (180g) all purpose flour
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • 1 cup (175g) chocolate chips

Steps:

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. Make the Brownie Layer:
    1. In a bowl, whisk together the melted butter and granulated sugar until smooth.
    2. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, and whisk until well combined.
    3. Stir in the cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder. Mix until smooth.
    4. Pour the brownie batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly.
  3. Make the Cookie Dough Layer:
    1. In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
    2. Add the egg and vanilla extract, and mix until combined.
    3. n another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
    4. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  4. Spoon the cookie dough over the brownie layer, gently spreading it to cover the top. It’s okay if it’s not perfectly even.
  5. Bake the brookie in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with only a few moist crumbs (not wet batter).
  6. Let the brookie cool in the pan for about 15 minutes before cutting it into squares. You can serve it warm, or allow it to cool completely.
